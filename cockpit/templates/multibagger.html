How to use the Conviction column  — it's a holding monitor, not a stop-loss

The edge in multibaggers is not getting shaken out of a winner during its inevitable drawdown — 81% of eventual 3×+ winners fall ≥30% en route, so a price stop-loss ejects you from the very names you're trying to hold. Use this column to reassess each holding on a rolling 3–6 month cadence, not daily.

HOLD  Near its high, or sector momentum and relative strength still intact. Action: do nothing — let the winner run.
WATCH  In a drawdown (roughly −25% to −50%) with mixed signals. Action: hold, but monitor — re-read the thesis; add only on strength, don't cut on the dip alone.
REVIEW  The full loser signature: deep (≤−50%) + ≥6 months underwater + sector momentum rolled over + underperforming the market, and the broad small-cap market isn't itself in a bear (so the weakness is idiosyncratic). Action: reassess the thesis — this is a flag to dig in, NOT a mechanical sell.

Why REVIEW is "reassess", not "sell". A 15-year bear stress test (2008 GFC, 2011–13, 2015–16, 2020 COVID) showed deeply beaten-down names mean-revert hard far more often than they die — an un-guarded eject would have sold IT at the 2008 bottom and PSU Banks at the COVID bottom, both of which then ~doubled. So REVIEW is deliberately rare and conservative: it auto-suppresses during a market-wide small-cap bear (only an idiosyncratic decline qualifies), and even when it fires you should confirm with the fundamentals — broken thesis, governance red flag, debt blow-up — before acting.
